Finding Stillness in a World That Never Stops Moving

Oct 24, 2023 6 min read
Photo by Unsplash

The morning sun filtered through the curtains, casting long, lazy shadows across the floorboards. It was one of those rare moments where time seemed to suspend itself, holding its breath just for me. In our modern lives, these pockets of silence are becoming endangered species.

We are constantly bombarded by notifications, demands, and the relentless scroll of information. The concept of "Alghurobaa"—the strangers—feels more relevant than ever. To choose stillness is to choose to be a stranger to the chaos. It is an act of quiet rebellion.

The Art of Disconnection

Yesterday, I left my phone in the drawer for three hours. The anxiety was immediate. My hand twitched, reaching for a phantom device in my pocket. But as the minutes ticked by, the anxiety was replaced by a profound sense of clarity. I noticed the way the light hit the coffee cup. I heard the distant hum of the city not as noise, but as a backdrop to my own thoughts.

"Solitude is not the absence of company, but the moment when our soul is free to speak to us and help us decide what to do with our life."

Reconnecting with oneself requires intentional friction. We must build barriers against the easy dopamine hits. We must learn to sit with boredom until it blossoms into creativity. It is not easy. It is a practice, much like meditation or exercise.

As I sat there, simply breathing, I realized that the world didn't end because I wasn't watching it. The emails would wait. The news cycle would spin without my participation. For a brief hour, I was just a human being, being.
